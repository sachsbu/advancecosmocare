Science of Fading

How Advanced Laser Tattoo Removal Works

We leverage advanced energy-based Q-switched lasers to safely target tattoo pigments at different depths.

1. Pigment Targeting

Selective Wavelengths

Specific wavelengths of laser light are absorbed only by the target ink colors in the dermis, keeping surrounding skin structures safe.

2. Photoacoustic Shattering

Energy Pulses

The laser delivers short, high-energy pulses in nanoseconds. This speed shatters ink particles into a micro-dust format without causing heat damage to outer layers.

3. Lymphatic Elimination

Natural Immune Fading

Once broken down, the body's natural scavenger cells (macrophages) ingest the micro-particles and carry them away via the lymphatic system over the next 6 to 8 weeks.

Clinical Protocols

Your Treatment Journey

Every clinical laser session is conducted under strict safety guidelines for comfortable, optimal outcomes.

Step 1

Clinical Evaluation

Dr. Ankita Patel evaluates the age, depth, density, and colors of your tattoo to design a custom laser protocol.

Step 2

Skin Preparation

The target area is cleansed. A topical anesthetic numbing cream can be applied to ensure maximum comfort during the session.

Step 3

Laser Application

Laser energy pulses sweep across the tattoo outlines. You will see a temporary "frosting" effect, indicating the ink particles have broken apart.

Step 4

Soothing & Cooling

A soothing clinical ointment and a cooling pad are applied immediately to calm warmth, reduce redness, and protect the skin barrier.

Step 5

Post-Session Protection

A medical bandage or sterile dressing is applied, along with instructions on moisturizing and sun protection to ensure healthy healing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the treatment process, comfort, and efficacy.

Most tattoos require multiple sessions, depending on the tattoo's size, color, depth, age, and individual skin response. On average, a series of 4 to 8 sessions is required, spaced 6 to 8 weeks apart to allow the lymphatic system to clear the ink particles.
When performed under the professional guidance of a qualified cosmetologist like Dr. Ankita Patel, the risk of scarring is extremely low. The laser targets only the ink pigment, keeping the surrounding healthy skin intact.
Patients often describe the sensation as similar to the snap of a rubber band against the skin. We apply topical numbing creams or localized cooling systems to minimize discomfort and ensure a highly tolerable procedure.
Black and dark blue inks are the easiest to remove because they absorb all laser wavelengths. Green, red, and yellow inks are more stubborn but can be effectively faded using specialized Q-switched laser wavelengths.
