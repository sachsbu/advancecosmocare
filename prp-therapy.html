PRP Preparation & Treatment

Treatment Description

How PRP is Administered

A small amount of your blood is drawn and processed to concentrate the platelet-rich plasma. The PRP is then carefully applied or injected into the targeted area to encourage natural healing and regeneration.

Clinical Parameters

Session Information

Session Duration: Approx. 45–60 minutes per treatment (including centrifugation preparation).
Recommended Cycle: Series of 4–6 sessions, spaced 4 weeks apart, for optimal structural improvement.

Clinical Protocols

Your Treatment Journey

Every clinical Platelet-Rich Plasma procedure follows high sterilization and calibration parameters.

Step 1

Clinical Consultation

Dr. Ankita Patel reviews your scalp density or skin concerns, checking clinical eligibility and setting session goals.

Step 2

Blood Collection

A small amount of blood (typically 10-15 ml) is drawn from your arm into sterile, specialized vacuum tubes.

Step 3

Centrifugation Separation

The blood tubes are placed in a high-speed clinical centrifuge. This splits red blood cells from the light, growth-factor-dense Platelet-Rich Plasma layers.

Step 4

PRP Inoculation

The concentrated platelets are extracted. Using precise micro-needles, the PRP is carefully injected or microneedled directly into targeted scalp or facial layers.

Step 5

Soothing & Recovery

A cooling ice compress or healing moisturizer is applied. You receive post-care instructions to preserve the follicle stimulation cycle.
